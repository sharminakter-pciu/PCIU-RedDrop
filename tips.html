Blood Donation Tips

Before Donating Blood

  • Eat a healthy meal and stay hydrated.
  • Avoid fatty foods before donation.
  • Get a good night's sleep before donating.
  • Avoid alcohol consumption at least 24 hours prior.
  • Wear comfortable clothing with sleeves that can be rolled up.

During Donation

  • Relax and remain calm.
  • Inform the staff if you feel any discomfort or dizziness.
  • Follow the instructions of the medical staff carefully.

After Donating Blood

  • Rest for at least 10-15 minutes.
  • Drink plenty of fluids to replenish lost fluids.
  • Avoid heavy physical activity or exercise for the rest of the day.
  • Keep the bandage on for a few hours to prevent bleeding.
  • If you feel unwell, inform medical staff immediately.

Who Should NOT Donate Blood

  • Individuals with infectious diseases (e.g., HIV, hepatitis).
  • Those currently feeling ill or with a fever.
  • People who have recently undergone surgery or major dental work.
  • Individuals who have traveled to certain countries recently (check local guidelines).
  • Women who are pregnant or breastfeeding.
  • People with anemia or low hemoglobin levels.
  • Those taking certain medications (consult a doctor).
